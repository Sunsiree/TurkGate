Welcome to TurkGate.

TurkGate, or Grouping and Access Tools for External surveys (for use with Amazon Mechanical Turk), is a software library that enables better control of access to external surveys. TurkGate was created for the purpose of conducting psychological experiments, and thus includes research-oriented features not included with Amazon Mechanical Turk.

What does TurkGate do?

TurkGate comprises three major features:

  1. Assigning surveys to groups
  2. Restricting survey previews
  3. Verifying survey completion

First and foremost, TurkGate allows you group HITs together, such that workers may only access one survey within a group. As soon as a worker has accessed a survey in a particular group, they are denied future access to that survey, or any other surveys in the same group. In certain research settings, allowing a participant to access similar surveys (e.g., different versions of a study) is unacceptable. Amazon Mechanical Turk does not offer a solution for such issues.

Likewise, it is usually the case that researchers cannot allow workers to see any meaningful part of a survey prior to committing to it. Hence, TurkGate enables the absolute restriction of survey previews. It also prevents workers from returning to a survey (even if they closed it accidentally).

Finally, TurkGate provides a method for assigning completion codes to workers after they finish your surveys. Rather than using a static (i.e., insecure) code, TurkGate generates dynamic and encrypted codes that can later be validated automatically.

How does TurkGate Work?

TurkGate provides researchers with templates for using Mechanical Turk via the web interface or the command line tools. Upon installation, TurkGate initializes a database on your server to track workers, surveys, and groups.
